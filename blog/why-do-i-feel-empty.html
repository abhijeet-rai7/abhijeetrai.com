Why Do I Feel Empty Even When Everything Is Fine?

On paper, things are okay. Maybe even good. The job, the relationship, the place to live, the things you once wanted. And still, in a quiet moment, there's a hollow feeling you can't explain — and a guilt on top of it, because what right do you have to feel empty when you have so much?

Here's something the books on "more" never tell you.

Look honestly at how the wanting works. You wanted the thing. You got it. For a little while, relief — and then the wanting quietly moved on to the next thing. It always does. The promotion, the move, the relationship: each one promised to be the thing that finally fills you, and each one, once it arrived, handed the job to the next.

We're taught the emptiness is a gap — something missing that the right achievement, person, or purchase will fill. So we spend our lives feeding a hunger that gets hungry again every few hours, sure the next bite is the one that lasts.

But what if the emptiness isn't a hole to be filled?

What if it's a pointer — quietly telling you that you've been looking for yourself in things that can never be you? Every time you got what you wanted, the "you" that wanted it was still there, still restless, already looking past it. Maybe the problem was never that you didn't have enough. Maybe it's that you've been looking in the wrong direction, for the wrong thing.

I won't hand you the answer in a paragraph — said too fast, it sounds like a fridge magnet, and you'd nod and forget it. But I'll say this: that hollow feeling is not a sign something is wrong with you. It's one of the most honest things in you. It's the part that refuses to be fooled by the next shiny thing. It's worth listening to — not numbing.

Where it points, when you finally follow it instead of running from it, is the quiet centre of my book.
